# F45 Training

## What F45 Is
F45 Training is a 45-minute group fitness concept founded in Australia in 2012. "F" stands for Functional, "45" is the session duration. It combines circuit training and HIIT across a rotating library of 45+ named workouts. Classes are screen-driven — exercises are displayed on TVs around the gym rather than called out by an instructor. Athletes rotate through stations in small groups. The format creates a team atmosphere despite individual effort.

## Session Types
F45 has three session categories — select one appropriate to the request:

**Cardio Sessions** (e.g. Hollywood, Angry Bird, Rome, Tokyo)
- Higher intensity, faster-paced
- Cardio equipment + bodyweight explosive movements
- Shorter work intervals, less rest
- 45s work / 15s rest per station is typical
- Exercises: rowing intervals, ski erg, assault bike, box jumps, burpees, mountain climbers, jump rope, lateral bounds, battle ropes

**Resistance Sessions** (e.g. Lions, Bears, Panthers, Troy)
- Strength-focused
- Free weights, barbells, dumbbells, kettlebells
- Slightly longer rest, heavier loads
- 40s work / 20s rest common
- Exercises: barbell deadlifts, DB bench press, KB swings, goblet squats, bent-over rows, push press, split squats, hip thrusts

**Hybrid Sessions** (e.g. Firestorm, Athletica, Revolution)
- Mix of cardio and resistance
- Alternates between cardio and strength stations
- Most common format — combines both exercise banks above

## Session Structure
F45 uses a pod/station rotation system. A typical 45-minute session:

**Standard format (27 stations × 2 rounds):**
- 3 pods of 9 stations
- Each pod performed twice before moving to the next
- 45s work / 15s transition per station
- Total working time: ~36–40 min
- 3–5 min warm-up + cool-down

**Simplified format for generation:**
Build 6–9 distinct exercises and arrange them into 3 rounds (each exercise hit twice). Use `rounds` or `amrap` format for each pod. Keep rest short — 15–20s between exercises, 30s between pods.

## Coaching Style
- Screen-driven — exercises shown on monitors, athletes self-direct within the station
- Coach circulates to correct form and motivate, not to call exercises
- All athletes working simultaneously — high energy group atmosphere
- Time-based intervals keep everyone on the same clock
- Modifications shown on screen alongside the main movement
- Weights suggested on screen per exercise — always prescribe specific weights

## Key Characteristics
- Station-based rotation is the defining format — multiple exercises, each done for a fixed time
- Sessions feel varied and never boring — no exercise is repeated too many times in a session
- Short work intervals (40–45s) with very short rest (10–20s) keep heart rate elevated
- Cardio and resistance movements are often interspersed even on "resistance" days
- The 45-minute duration is fixed — workouts are tight and efficient with no wasted time
- Community and team feel despite individual training
