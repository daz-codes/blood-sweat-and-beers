# Functional Fitness

## Training Philosophy
Movement quality and broad conditioning using gym equipment. The goal is sport performance, injury resilience, and general physical preparedness — not competing in a specific race format. Sessions combine strength and conditioning in the same workout. Prefer compound, multi-joint movements over isolation work. Think: things a strong, capable human should be able to do.

## Session Formats
- **Rounds** — structured circuit with planned rest. Core format for functional fitness.
- **AMRAP** — clock-driven conditioning for mixed modal circuits.
- **EMOM** — great for strength skill work with built-in pacing.
- **For Time** — use for benchmark efforts and finishers.
- **Straight** — isolated strength sets with full rest (e.g. heavy carries, deadlift work).

## Movement Vocabulary

**Kettlebells**
KB Swing (Russian and American), KB Clean, KB Snatch, KB Press, KB Turkish Get-up (TGU), KB Goblet Squat, KB Lunge, KB Windmill, KB Row — kettlebells are a cornerstone of functional fitness. Use them often.

**Sleds and carries**
Sled Push, Sled Pull (belt or strap), Farmer's Carry (KB, DB, or trap bar), Front-Rack Carry, Overhead Carry, Sandbag Carry, Yoke Carry — loaded carries build genuine total-body strength.

**Battle ropes and cables**
Battle Ropes (alternating waves, slams, spirals), Cable Row, Cable Face Pull, Landmine Press, Landmine Rotation, TRX Row, TRX Push-up, TRX Fallout

**Dumbbells**
DB Romanian Deadlift, DB Bench Press, DB Row, DB Shoulder Press, DB Reverse Lunge, DB Step-up, DB Lateral Raise

**Bodyweight and gymnastics**
Ring Row, Ring Dip, Pull-ups, Chin-ups, Push-ups, Dips, Box Jumps, Box Step-overs, Broad Jumps, Plank Variations, L-sit

**Medicine balls**
Wall Ball, Med Ball Slam, Med Ball Russian Twist, Med Ball Clean

**Machines (use sparingly for intervals or warm-ups)**
Rowing, SkiErg, Assault Bike — great for conditioning intervals within a functional circuit.

## Session Structure
Warm-up → Main circuit (2–4 blocks, each with a distinct focus) → Optional finisher → Cool-down stretches.

Mix strength exercises with conditioning work in the same session — e.g. heavy KB carries followed by rowing intervals followed by ring rows.

## Coaching Cues
- Focus on quality of movement, not just load. Cue tempo on key lifts (e.g. "3-second eccentric on the RDL").
- Carries should be heavy enough to engage the lats and feel the core working.
- TGUs should be slow and controlled — they're a skill drill, not a conditioning movement.
- Rows and pulls balance the pressing work — include pulling in every session.
