# Deka Atlas

## Format
Deka Atlas is a heavy, shoulder-intensive 10-zone functional fitness competition with a **30-minute time cap**. Zero running. Athletes complete the 10 zones back-to-back in order.

**Sequence:** Zone 1 → Zone 2 → … → Zone 10 (no running between zones)

## The 10 Zones

Weights shown are for the **Peak division**, ages 17–59. Foundation division uses lighter weights (~30–50% reduction). Ages 14–16 and 60+ use Foundation weights.

| Zone | Exercise | Reps / Distance | Men (Peak) | Women (Peak) |
|------|----------|-----------------|------------|--------------|
| 1 | Barbell Thrusters | 20 reps | 45kg | 30kg |
| 2 | Bar-Facing Burpees Over Bar | 20 reps | — | — |
| 3 | Surrender Lunges (weighted) | 20 reps | 22.5kg | 15kg |
| 4 | Single Arm DB Ground to Overhead (alternating) | 20 reps | 22.5kg | 15kg |
| 5 | Dumbbell Bear Crawl | 40m | 22.5kg | 15kg |
| 6 | Weighted Sit-ups | 20 reps | 15kg | 9kg |
| 7 | Farmer's Carry | 60m | 45kg each hand | 32kg each hand |
| 8 | DB Shoulder to Overhead Press (both DBs) | 20 reps | 22.5kg | 15kg |
| 9 | Jump Rope Single Unders | 100 reps | — | — |
| 10 | Atlas Shoulder to Carry | 100m | 45kg | 32kg |

*Verify current weights and specifications at spartan.com/en/deka/atlas — standards may be updated.*

## Training Philosophy
Training sessions prepare athletes for Deka Atlas — they are **not** mini copies of the event. The defining challenge is **shoulder fatigue under sustained load**, so sessions should develop overhead strength and endurance, grip, core stability, and the ability to keep working with accumulated shoulder fatigue.

The 10 official zones are the primary training tools. Sessions do **not** need to include all 10 zones — focused blocks targeting 2–5 zones are often more effective. Complementary exercises are appropriate when they target the same physical demands: e.g. DB overhead press, push press, TGU, lateral raises, or overhead holds for shoulder endurance; heavy KB carries or trap bar walks for carry capacity; hollow body holds or GHD sit-ups for core. Avoid exercises completely unrelated to the event's demands.

## Training Principles
- **Shoulder fatigue is the defining challenge** — Zones 1, 4, 5, 8, and 10 all load the shoulders
- **30-minute time cap** — this is a race against the clock, not just for time
- **Barbell thrusters opener** — Zone 1 is demanding; opening too hot destroys later zones
- **Grip + carry** — Farmer's Carry at 45kg/hand for 60m and Atlas Carry for 100m are major limiters
- **Bear crawl pacing** — 40m of loaded bear crawl with fatigued shoulders is brutal; go steadily

## Common Training Session Types
1. **Full simulation** — all 10 zones back-to-back (reduce weights 20–30% for training runs)
2. **Shoulder superset blocks** — Thrusters + DB G2OH + Bear Crawl in rotation
3. **Barbell strength focus** — Thruster volume with accessory pressing
4. **Carry circuits** — Farmer's Carry + Atlas Carry + Bear Crawl
5. **Zone 1–5 block** — first half of the event at near-competition weight
6. **Zone 6–10 block** — second half, emphasising carry endurance and overhead fatigue

## Example Sessions

**Session: Zone 1–5 Simulation**
- 20 Barbell Thrusters (45/30kg) → 20 Bar-Facing Burpees → 20 Surrender Lunges (22.5/15kg)
- → 20 Single Arm DB G2OH (22.5/15kg) → 40m DB Bear Crawl
- Rest 4 min. Repeat 1–2 times at reduced weight.

**Session: Shoulder & Carry Endurance**
- 4 rounds: 60m Farmer's Carry (45/32kg per hand) → 20 DB Shoulder Press (22.5/15kg) → 100m Atlas Carry (45/32kg)
- Rest 3 min between rounds

**Session: Burpee + Jump Rope Conditioning**
- 5 rounds: 10 Bar-Facing Burpees Over Bar → 50 Jump Rope Single Unders → 10 Weighted Sit-ups (15/9kg)
- Rest 90s between rounds
