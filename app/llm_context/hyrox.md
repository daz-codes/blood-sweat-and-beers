# Hyrox

## Race Format
Hyrox is a global fitness race: 8km of running (8 × 1km laps) interleaved with 8 functional fitness stations in a fixed order.

**Sequence:** 1km run → Station 1 → 1km run → Station 2 → ... → 1km run → Station 8

## The 8 Stations (race order)

| # | Station | Open Men | Open Women | Pro Men | Pro Women |
|---|---------|----------|------------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | SkiErg | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m |
| 2 | Sled Push | 50m / 152kg | 50m / 102kg | 50m / 202kg | 50m / 152kg |
| 3 | Sled Pull | 50m / 103kg | 50m / 78kg | 50m / 153kg | 50m / 103kg |
| 4 | Burpee Broad Jumps | 80m | 80m | 80m | 80m |
| 5 | Rowing | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m |
| 6 | Farmers Carry | 200m / 2×24kg | 200m / 2×16kg | 200m / 2×32kg | 200m / 2×24kg |
| 7 | Sandbag Lunges | 100m / 20kg | 100m / 10kg | 100m / 30kg | 100m / 20kg |
| 8 | Wall Balls | 100 reps / 6kg to 10ft | 100 reps / 4kg to 9ft | 100 reps / 9kg to 10ft | 100 reps / 6kg to 9ft |

## Categories
- **Open:** Standard weights above
- **Pro:** Heavier loads (see table), for elite athletes
- **Doubles (same-sex and mixed):** Partners share all work, alternating freely within each station

## Training Philosophy
Training sessions prepare athletes for Hyrox — they are **not** mini copies of the race. Sessions should develop the physical qualities the race demands: aerobic capacity, running economy, grip endurance, posterior chain strength, and the ability to work hard immediately after running.

The 8 race stations are the primary training tools but not the only ones. Complementary exercises are appropriate when they target the same demands — e.g. box step-ups or DB lunges for leg endurance, RDLs or KB swings for posterior chain, ring rows or DB bent-over rows for sled pulling muscles, goblet squats for wall ball prep. Sessions do **not** need to include all 8 stations. A focused 3-station block or a run-heavy session is often more useful than a full race simulation.

## Training Principles
- **Running fitness is critical** — 8km total broken into 1km splits with no rest
- **Station-specific technique** — each station has its own pacing and movement strategy
- **Transition training** — running into a station without a recovery break is a key skill
- **Race simulation** — partial or full run-throughs build specificity; reduce to 20–30% of race volume for training runs

## Common Training Session Types
1. **Running intervals** — 8×1km, 400m repeats, tempo runs targeting 1km split pace
2. **Station isolation** — high volume on one or two stations with running transitions
3. **Combo blocks** — e.g. 400m run → 250m SkiErg × 4, or run → sled → run
4. **Full mock race** — reduced weights/distances, all 8 stations in sequence
5. **Strength accessory** — posterior chain, grip, and upper back for sled and carry stations

## Station Technique Notes
- **SkiErg:** Full hip extension, arms pull through to hips, not just shoulder height. Drive with legs, not just arms.
- **Sled Push:** Low position, drive with legs, short fast steps. Head down, eyes forward.
- **Sled Pull:** Keep core braced, alternate hand-over-hand or shuffle walk backwards.
- **Burpee Broad Jumps:** Set a rhythm, land softly, jump for consistent distance every rep.
- **Row:** 18–22 strokes/min for Hyrox pacing, damper typically 4–5.
- **Farmers Carry:** Tall posture, quick turnover, don't over-grip (wastes energy).
- **Sandbag Lunges:** Sandbag on shoulder or front rack, big lunges, maintain upright torso.
- **Wall Balls:** Full squat depth, drive through heels, catch the ball high. Broken sets are acceptable.
