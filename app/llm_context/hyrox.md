# Hyrox

## Race Format
Hyrox is a global fitness race: 8km of running (8 × 1km laps) interleaved with 8 functional fitness stations in a fixed order.

**Sequence:** 1km run → Station 1 → 1km run → Station 2 → ... → 1km run → Station 8

## The 8 Stations (race order)

| # | Station | Open Men | Open Women | Pro Men | Pro Women |
|---|---------|----------|------------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | SkiErg | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m |
| 2 | Sled Push | 50m / 152kg | 50m / 102kg | 50m / 202kg | 50m / 152kg |
| 3 | Sled Pull | 50m / 103kg | 50m / 78kg | 50m / 153kg | 50m / 103kg |
| 4 | Burpee Broad Jumps | 80m | 80m | 80m | 80m |
| 5 | Rowing | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m | 1000m |
| 6 | Farmers Carry | 200m / 2×24kg | 200m / 2×16kg | 200m / 2×32kg | 200m / 2×24kg |
| 7 | Sandbag Lunges | 100m / 20kg | 100m / 10kg | 100m / 30kg | 100m / 20kg |
| 8 | Wall Balls | 100 reps / 6kg to 10ft | 100 reps / 4kg to 9ft | 100 reps / 9kg to 10ft | 100 reps / 6kg to 9ft |

## Training Philosophy
Training sessions prepare athletes for Hyrox — they are **not** mini copies of the race. Build aerobic capacity, running economy under fatigue, grip endurance, posterior chain strength, and the ability to work hard immediately after running.

The 8 race stations are the primary training tools but not the only ones. Sessions do **not** need all 8 stations — a focused 3-station block or a run-heavy session is often more useful than a full race simulation. Complementary exercises are welcome: box step-ups or DB lunges for leg endurance, KB swings for posterior chain, ring rows for sled pulling muscles. Avoid exercises unrelated to the event's demands.
