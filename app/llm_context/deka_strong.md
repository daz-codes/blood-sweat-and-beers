# Deka Strong

## Format
Deka Strong uses the same 10 DEKA zones as Deka Fit and Deka Mile, but with **no running**. Athletes complete the 10 zones back-to-back in order as quickly as possible.

**Sequence:** Zone 1 → Zone 2 → Zone 3 → … → Zone 10 (no running between zones)

## The 10 Zones

| Zone | Exercise | Reps / Distance | Men | Women |
|------|----------|-----------------|-----|-------|
| 1 | RAM Reverse Lunges (alternating) | 30 reps (15/leg) | 25kg | 15kg |
| 2 | Row | 500m | — | — |
| 3 | Box Jump / Step Over | 20 reps | 24" box | 24" box |
| 4 | Med Ball Sit-up Throw | 25 reps | 9kg | 6.5kg |
| 5 | SkiErg | 500m | — | — |
| 6 | Farmer's Carry | 100m | 27kg each hand | 18kg each hand |
| 7 | Air Bike | 25 calories | — | — |
| 8 | Dead Ball Yoke Over | 20 reps (10/side) | 27kg | 18kg |
| 9 | Sled Push / Pull | 100m (push 10m + pull 10m × 5) | resistance varies | resistance varies |
| 10 | RAM Weighted Burpees | 20 reps | 20kg | 10kg |

*Verify current weights and specifications at spartan.com/en/deka/strong — standards may be updated.*

## Training Philosophy
Training sessions prepare athletes for Deka Strong — they are **not** mini copies of the event. Sessions should develop the physical qualities the event demands: posterior chain strength and endurance, grip, leg power, aerobic capacity, and the ability to sustain work across 10 back-to-back stations.

The 10 official zones are the primary training tools. Sessions do **not** need to include all 10 zones — focused blocks of 2–5 zones are often more effective. Complementary exercises are appropriate when they target the same physical demands: e.g. goblet squats or step-ups for lunge/leg work, DB rows or ring rows for rowing muscles, KB swings or RDLs for posterior chain, push-ups or DB press for burpee strength. Avoid exercises completely unrelated to the event's demands.

## Training Principles
- **No running** — this is the key differentiator from Deka Fit and Deka Mile
- **Zone pacing** — going out too hard on the lunge or carry zones causes failure later
- **Grip endurance** — farmer's carry at 27kg/hand for 100m is a significant limiter
- **SkiErg mid-event** — Zone 5 SkiErg comes at the midpoint; pace it as active recovery
- **Posterior chain fatigue** — RAM lunge, row, sled, and carry all load the posterior chain

## Common Training Session Types
1. **Full Deka Strong simulation** — all 10 zones back-to-back (reduced reps/distance for training)
2. **Zone blocks** — 3–5 zones in sequence
3. **Individual zone focus** — high volume on weak zones
4. **Grip + carry supersets** — farmer's carry + rowing intervals
5. **Conditioning chipper** — all 10 zones for time, single effort

## Example Sessions

**Session: Zone 1–5 Block**
- 30 RAM Reverse Lunges → 500m Row → 20 Box Jump Overs → 25 Med Ball Sit-up Throws → 500m SkiErg
- Rest 3 min. Repeat 2–3 times (reduce reps to 60–70% for repeats)

**Session: Grip & Carry Focus**
- 4 rounds: 100m Farmer's Carry (27/18kg) → 500m Row → 100m Sled Push/Pull
- Rest 2 min between rounds

**Session: Zone 7–10 Finisher**
- 3 rounds: 25 cal Air Bike → 20 Dead Ball Yoke Overs → 100m Sled Push/Pull → 10 RAM Weighted Burpees
- Rest 2 min between rounds
