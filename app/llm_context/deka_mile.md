# Deka Mile

## Format
Deka Mile emphasises running performance combined with 10 functional fitness stations. Running fitness is weighted more heavily than in Deka Fit, and running distances are longer.

*Verify current station specifications and distances at deka.life — formats may vary by event.*

## Structure
A mile run component is integrated with 10 functional stations. Athletes must maintain running performance alongside functional output. Sessions are typically paced by mile split time.

## Typical Stations
May include variations of: SkiErg intervals (500m–1000m), Rowing (500m–1000m), Bike Erg (500m–1000m), Burpee box jump overs, DB goblet squats, Sandbag cleans, Farmers carry, Running (longer distances than Deka Fit).

## Training Philosophy
Training sessions prepare athletes for Deka Mile — they are **not** mini copies of the event. Running economy is critical — mile pace is a key performance driver. Build aerobic capacity, running-to-station transition fitness, and pacing discipline. Sessions should combine running with functional station work; run-heavy sessions are often as valuable as station-focused blocks.
