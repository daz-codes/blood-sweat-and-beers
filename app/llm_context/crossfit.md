# CrossFit

## Training Philosophy
Functional movements performed at high intensity, constantly varied. No prescribed sequence — every session should feel different from the last. The stimulus matters: aerobic engine, strength, speed, or mixed modal. Sessions should feel hard, have a clear purpose, and be completable in the time cap.

## Session Formats
Use these actively — vary them between sections and across workouts:
- **AMRAP** — as many rounds/reps as possible in a time cap. Great for mixed modal conditioning (e.g. AMRAP 12: 10 thrusters + 10 pull-ups + 200m run).
- **For Time** — complete prescribed work as fast as possible, record finishing time. Great for benchmark efforts, chippers (e.g. 21-15-9, descending ladders).
- **EMOM** — every minute on the minute. Great for strength skill work or paced conditioning intervals (e.g. EMOM 10: 3 clean and jerks).
- **Tabata** — 20s work / 10s rest × 8 rounds = exactly 4 minutes. Great for max-effort cardio bursts.
- **Rounds** — structured circuit with planned rest. Good for strength-focused blocks with controlled recovery.

## Movement Vocabulary

**Barbell (compound strength)**
Clean, Power Clean, Hang Clean, Snatch, Hang Snatch, Deadlift, Romanian Deadlift, Back Squat, Front Squat, Thruster, Push Press, Push Jerk, Overhead Squat, Good Morning

**Gymnastics (bodyweight skill)**
Pull-ups, Chest-to-Bar Pull-ups, Toes-to-Bar, Kipping Swing, Muscle-up (bar or ring), Ring Dips, Handstand Push-up (HSPU), Handstand Walk, L-sit, Box Jumps, Broad Jumps

**Monostructural cardio**
Rowing, SkiErg, Assault Bike / Echo Bike — use for warm-ups, AMRAP elements, or interval work. These are machines, not running.

**Kettlebells**
KB Swing (Russian or American), KB Snatch, KB Clean + Press, KB Turkish Get-up (TGU), KB Goblet Squat, KB Farmer's Carry

**Dumbbells**
DB Hang Clean, DB Thruster, DB Snatch, DB Lunges, DB Push Press, DB Row, DB Bench Press

**Bodyweight / Gymnastics**
Push-ups, Pike Push-ups, Dips, Burpees, Jump Rope (single, double-under), Wall Walk, V-ups, Hollow Body Hold

## Session Structure
Warm-up → Strength/Skill block (optional, 10–15 min) → Main WOD (AMRAP, For Time, EMOM, or Tabata) → Cool-down stretches.

For shorter sessions (under 30 min): drop the strength block, go straight to the WOD.

## Coaching Cues
- Name the stimulus: "This is an aerobic grind — pace it." or "This is a sprint — go out hot."
- For For Time workouts: prescribe a realistic time cap (usually 15–25 min for mixed modal).
- For AMRAP workouts: suggest a rep/round target to aim for.
- Weight suggestions should challenge but allow unbroken sets (or minimal breaks) for most athletes.
