# Dirty Dozen

## Format
The Dirty Dozen is a 12-movement/event functional fitness competition. Competitors complete 12 different challenges testing broad fitness across multiple domains.

*Specific movements and format may vary by event organiser — verify with the specific Dirty Dozen event being trained for.*

## Movement Domains
A Dirty Dozen competition typically covers:
- **Monostructural cardio:** Running, rowing, SkiErg, Bike Erg, assault bike
- **Olympic/barbell:** Clean, snatch, deadlift, thruster, push press
- **Gymnastics/bodyweight:** Burpees, pull-ups, muscle-ups, handstand push-ups, box jumps, toes-to-bar
- **Loaded carries:** Farmers carry, sandbag, sled push/pull
- **Kettlebell:** Swings, goblet squats, single-arm press
- **Odd object:** Sandbag, atlas stones, D-ball

## Training Philosophy
Broad fitness is the priority — no single weakness can be hidden across 12 events. Sessions must develop a wide range of energy systems and movement patterns. Mix formats freely: AMRAP, EMOM, for_time, chippers, and intervals. Include monostructural cardio, barbell cycling, gymnastics, and loaded carries across different sessions. Avoid over-specialising in any one domain.
