# Warm-Up & Cool-Down Guidelines

## Warm-Up (always 5 minutes, format: straight, duration_mins: 5)

Pick ONE approach per session — vary it each time. Do not always use the same exercises.

### Option A — Easy Cardio + Dynamic Stretches (most common)
2 min easy cardio (row, jog, skip, jumping jacks, or bike — match the session type) then 3–4 dynamic movements:
- Hip circles / leg swings
- Arm circles / cross-body swings
- Inchworm to push-up
- World's greatest stretch (lunge + thoracic rotation)
- Walkout to downward dog
- Lateral shuffles or skips

### Option B — Joint Mobility Circuit
No cardio — move through the joints systematically. Good for strength days:
- Neck rolls → shoulder rolls → thoracic rotations
- Hip 90/90 switches
- Deep squat hold with thoracic reach
- Hip flexor stretch with reach
- Glute bridges (slow, 10 reps)
- Cat-cow → thread the needle

### Option C — Activation Circuit
Bodyweight primer to switch on the muscles you're about to use. 2 rounds of:
- Glute bridges or banded clamshells (glutes)
- Dead bugs or hollow hold (core activation)
- Band pull-aparts or face pulls (shoulders)
- Bodyweight squats with pause at bottom

### Option D — Sport-Specific Ramp-Up
Mini version of the session's main movements at low intensity:
- For lower body day: air squats → reverse lunges → jump squats (light)
- For upper body day: push-up negatives → scapular pull-ups → band rows
- For conditioning day: 1 min easy row or bike → high knees → lateral bounds
- For Olympic lifting: empty bar warm-up sets of the main movement (3×5 light)

### Option E — Flow Warm-Up
Continuous movement, no stopping. 5 min of:
- Jumping jacks → high knees → butt kicks → lateral shuffles → inchworms → walkouts → arm swings
- Flow from one to the next with no rest — builds body temperature quickly

## Cool-Down (always 5 minutes, format: straight, duration_mins: 5)

Always static stretches, 30–45s holds. No reps, no distances. Use exercise notes to describe the hold (e.g. "30s each side"). Pick from these — vary the selection per session:

**Hip & Glutes**
- Hip flexor stretch (kneeling lunge, front knee 90°) — 30s each side
- Pigeon pose or figure-four glute stretch — 45s each side
- Butterfly stretch (seated, soles together, lean forward) — 45s

**Hamstrings & Lower Back**
- Seated forward fold or standing toe touch — 45s
- Supine hamstring stretch (lying, leg pulled gently to chest) — 30s each leg
- Child's pose — 45s

**Chest & Shoulders**
- Chest opener (hands clasped behind back, gentle lift) — 30s
- Cross-body shoulder stretch — 30s each arm
- Doorframe pec stretch — 30s each side

**Thoracic & Upper Back**
- Thoracic rotation (quadruped, hand behind head, rotate open to ceiling) — 30s each side
- Thread the needle — 30s each side
- Foam roller thoracic extension (if available) — 60s

**Quads & Calves**
- Standing quad stretch — 30s each leg
- Calf stretch (heel off step or against wall) — 30s each leg
- Seated or lying spinal twist — 30s each side

**General Guidance**
- Pick 4–5 stretches that target the muscles most used in this session
- Prioritise what was actually worked — leg day → hip flexors, hamstrings, quads. Upper body → chest, shoulders, lats.
- Never use reps in cool-down. Hold times only, described in notes.
